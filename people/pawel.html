Intro.

My research is based on two elements: development of computer tools and their use to study biological systems important in medicine, industry and society. I focus on development of both coarse-grained and all-atom methods, which combined together provide better results with reasonable computational cost. In my studies I investigate structure, stability, dynamics and thermodynamics of various peptides, proteins, nucleic acids, and lipids including molecules connected to immunological response of the organism to pathogens and diseases. Investigation of influence of nanoparticles, such as carbon nanotubes, fullerenes and metal nanoclusters showed possible problems of everyday use of nanoparticles, while some substances derived from plants were found to have positive influence in abnormal protein behavior, such as fibril formation. Currently I focus on cutting-edge molecular dynamics studies of disulfide bonds in proteins including disulfide-bond breaking and formation during simulations to study their role, function, relevance and connection to diseases.
